SrovChlart app feature plan — rice market platform for Cambodian farmers and buyers

Phase 1 — launch (MVP)

Live rice price feed

Daily updated market prices for jasmine, white, and fragrant rice per kg — pulled from Phnom Penh and provincial markets. Farmers see fair price before negotiating. Khmer + EN

Harvest listing

Farmers post their available rice — quantity (kg/ton), rice variety, location, and asking price. Simple photo upload. Buyers can browse and contact directly.

Verified profiles

Farmers verify via phone number + province. Buyers verify with business registration. Trust badges displayed on all listings. Reduces scam risk on both sides.

In-app chat

Buyer and farmer negotiate directly in-app — no need to share personal phone numbers until a deal is agreed. Chat history saved for dispute resolution.

Phase 2 — growth

Price history chart

30/90-day price trends per rice variety. Farmers see when prices peak seasonally — empowering them to time their sale and not panic-sell at harvest.

Rating & review

After each transaction both sides rate each other (1–5 stars, short comment). Builds a reputation system that rewards honest traders and flags bad actors.

Price alerts

Farmer sets a target price per kg. App sends push notification when market price hits that level. Buyers can alert for when large supply is available in their region.

Province filter

Buyers filter listings by province (Kampong Cham, Prey Veng, Kandal etc.). Farmers see buyer demand in their area. Reduces transport cost and improves matching.

Phase 3 — scale

Transaction record

Confirmed deals are logged on-platform with price, quantity, date, and both parties. Farmers build a verifiable sales history — usable as collateral for future microloans from MFI partners.

Traceability report

Exporters and premium buyers (hotels, EU importers) get a supply chain report: farm location, harvest date, variety, and transaction history. Enables premium pricing and EU market access.

Cooperative group

Farmer cooperatives can create a group listing — pooling supply from 10–50 members to offer bulk quantities that attract larger exporters and better prices.
